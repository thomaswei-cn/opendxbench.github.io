OpenDxBench: Evaluating Multimodal Diagnostic Reasoning in Clinical Practice

OpenDxBench is the first benchmark dedicated to multimodal diagnostic reasoning. Unlike prior datasets that are often limited to factoid-style questions or text-only input, OpenDxBench integrates clinical narratives, medical images, and ICD-11–coded diagnoses, creating tasks that closely reflect real-world clinical practice.

Abstract

The use of Large Language Models (LLMs) for diagnostic decision-making has garnered growing interest. However, existing benchmarks for evaluating diagnostic capabilities are predominantly limited to textual modality, neglecting the essential visual information involved in diagnostic process, thereby failing to reflect the complexity of real-world clinical scenarios. To bridge this gap, we introduce \textbf{OpenDxBench}, a multimodal benchmark constructed from real-world case reports sourced from top-tier medical journals and curated clinical case report databases. It comprises 625 expert-validated cases, with an average of 2.79 medical images per case and a total of 1,042 standardized diagnoses annotated with ICD-11 codes. We evaluate 21 Large Multimodal Models (LMMs) and find that most achieve diagnostic accuracy scores below 50 out of 100. Further analysis reveals that both the models’ medical knowledge proficiency and their reasoning capabilities in multimodal scenarios are highly correlated with diagnostic performance.
